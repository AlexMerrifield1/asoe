# Fulfillment and Follow Up Process 2.0

**Purpose:** Transform project implementations into long-term strategic partnerships through quarterly Fulfillment and Follow-up (FF) calls and Bi-Annual Executive Briefing (EB) calls that provide genuine value, build C-suite relationships, identify customer needs and opportunities, and naturally enable subscription renewals and expansion.

---

## Client Types and Cadence

### Client Tiers

- **Strategic** - Will do $500k or more annually in services with us on current contracts, or are targeted for that amount of expansion revenue.
- **Target** - Clients that reasonably fit our description of the ideal customer, want to automate their business, and see us as a partner long term (long term subscription revenue)
- **Growth** - Clients that may not yet fit our ICP, but we took as a client to grow AE relationships, partnerships, or we see the client as capable of growing to fit our ICP in the next year.

### Proposed Cadence

#### Bi-Annual Executive Briefings

**Strategic and Target Tier Clients:**
- Client Attendees - Client Executives (C-suite, VP, etc.), should be financial decision makers above project resources that can speak to their company's financial goals and strategic priorities
- SOLVD Attendees - VP Sales, Practice Director, CEO (optional)

**Growth Clients:**
- By request only

#### Quarterly Fulfillment and Follow Up (FF)

**Strategic and Target Clients:**
- Client Audience - Client Project Lead and Exec. Sponsor (optional but recommended)
- SOLVD Attendee - VP Sales or Practice Director

**Growth Clients:**
- Client Audience - Client Project Lead and Exec. Sponsor (optional but recommended)
- SOLVD Attendee - Consulting Manager

## Schedule EB Calls and FF Calls

**When:** During closing call (or project kickoff?)
**Who:** Opportunity Owner

### Actions:

1. **Pre-schedule the cadence during the deal close or kickoff meeting**
   - Script: *"As part of our partnership, we schedule an executive briefing with your leadership team (twice per year) to ensure alignment and share strategic insights on automation trends affecting your industry. We also have a quarterly alignment call to ensure we are meeting or exceeding your expectations and needs. That is a more tactical project call."*

2. **Block calendar holds for the next 6 months of calls**
   - Align EB calls as much as possible to THEIR quarterly business meetings or strategic plannings, not to how long our project has been going on. This way we are able to align our projects to their planning cycles. Book 45 mins.
   - FF calls should align to key project points. For shorter projects (less than 4 months) we target the end of build. For longer projects (more than 4 months) we will likely want a touch when A&D is done and gets approved, and at the end of build moving into testing.
   - All Strategic and Target clients should have *at least* one of each call scheduled at closing. Longer projects should have them scheduled through the duration. Growth clients will only have FF.

3. **Confirm participants:** Identify right participants on both sides and get them on the invite when it's created.
   - Consider having Adrian or an automation do follow up as these approach to confirm attendance.

4. **Set format preference:** Digital (default) for most but push for in-person for Utah-based accounts and Strategic Accounts

5. **Document in CRM**
   - What is proper need for tracking here
   - Report on upcoming FF and EB calls
   - Track which accounts have had them and how long it's been
   - Add this reporting to sales dashboard to ensure long term consistency

## EB Call Process

### Quarterly Research & Presentation Creation for EB Calls

**When:** First 2 weeks after Salesforce FQ end (Feb, May, Aug, Nov)
**Who:** VP of Sales (Bianca - owner)

#### First Draft - Offshore Assistant

1. **Mine our data for insights**
   - Sales Conversations Mining - use AI to surface trends from customers by industry
   - Client conversation Mining - Use AI to review notes from all client conversations from previous quarter and surface patterns or trend by industry
   - Identify recurring themes: challenges, questions, competitor mentions, adoption wins
   - Identify cross-industry patterns and sector-specific trends
   - Document specific client quotes and success stories (with permission)

2. **Industry Scanning**
   - Review key publications, blogs, industry reports (automation/AI lens)
   - Note major tech announcements, regulatory changes, market shifts
   - Compile competitor intelligence (what are clients' competitors doing in our focus industries?)

3. **Internal Insights**
   - Gather win stories from delivery team (adoption stats, ROI metrics, team feedback quotes)
   - Document new automation capabilities or solutions developed

4. **Create First Draft Master Deck**
   - 10-12 slide deck with high-level findings
   - Structure: Macro trends → Industry pressures → Automation opportunities → Cross-industry innovations
   - Keep modular (slides can be mixed/matched per industry)

#### Review and Enhancement of EB Deck - Practice Directors & CEO

1. Practice Directors add technical depth and specific solution insights
2. CEO adds strategic perspective and market intelligence
3. Focus on actionable recommendations clients can implement

#### Finalize Deck - VP of Sales

1. VP of Sales incorporates all feedback
2. Create final 12-15 slide master deck
3. Ensure clear narrative flow
4. Create Industry Templates
5. Adapt master deck for the four core industries:
   - High-Tech & SaaS
   - Financial Services (especially wealth management)
   - Consumer Goods
   - Professional Services
6. Customize with sector-specific data points, competitor examples, regulatory considerations
7. Save as templates for rapid client customization

**Deliverable:** Master insight deck + 4 industry-specific template versions ready for customization, saved to google drive and posted to the leadership slack channel

## Best Practices For EB Calls

### Lead with Value:
- Focus on their success and strategic insights
- Don't pitch or sell overtly—let opportunities emerge naturally

### Listen More Than Talk:
- In Q&A, actively listen to their priorities, concerns, and ideas
- Take detailed notes on their feedback and strategic direction
- Ask follow-up questions to understand their evolving needs

### Engage All Participants:
- If multiple executives attend, invite each to share their perspective
- *"Sarah, as COO, how does this align with your operational priorities?"*

### Stay on Time:
- 45 minutes total—respect their schedule
- If conversation is flowing, offer to extend but don't run over without permission

### ALWAYS SET THE NEXT ONE
- Don't leave without ensuring the next EB is scheduled

## Immediately After the Meeting

1. **Log findings**
   - What resonated most? What fell flat?
   - What did we learn about their evolving priorities?
   - Potential expansion opportunities identified?
   - Any concerns or red flags?
   - Document key takeaways in CRM as appropriate (new opps, notes, etc.)
   - Send key takeaways to project team as appropriate

2. **Send Follow-Up Email (within 24 hours)**
   - Thank executives for their time
   - Attach deck as PDF
   - Summarize 2-3 key takeaways from the discussion
   - Confirm any next steps or action items mentioned
   - NO hard sales language—keep it consultative and relationship-focused

### Sample Email Template

```
Hi [Executive Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with us yesterday. It was great to hear about [specific priority or insight they shared] and how your thinking has evolved around [topic].

A few key takeaways from our conversation:
- [Takeaway 1 related to their priorities]
- [Takeaway 2 about industry trend they reacted to]
- [Takeaway 3 about potential next step or exploration area]

I've attached the deck we reviewed. As discussed, we'll [specific action item, if any].

Looking forward to continuing the conversation at our next briefing in [Month].

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## EB Presentation Format Overview

1. **Start with appropriate industry template** (High-Tech, Financial Services, Consumer Goods, or Professional Services)

2. **Section 1 - Wins (2-3 slides):**
   - Specific project updates and milestones achieved - what we said and what we DID
   - 3-5 data points: usage stats, time saved, efficiency gains
   - 2-3 team member quotes (with permission): "This has transformed how we work…"
   - Visual: Before/after process diagram or metric comparison
   - *Talking points: Keep this factual and celebratory. Show you're paying attention and invested in their success. This isn't about SOLVD—it's about THEM winning and automating.*

3. **Section 2 - Industry Insights (3-4 slides):**
   - Tailor macro trends to their specific competitors/market position
   - Include relevant news, regulatory changes, or tech announcements
   - Highlight what we're seeing across our client base in their industry
   - One provocative insight or "you should be thinking about this" item
   - *Talking points: Don't lecture—share insights conversationally. Tie everything back to "why this matters for you." Invite reactions and questions as you go.*

4. **Section 3 - Priorities & Recommendations (2-3 slides):**
   - Review their original stated priorities from kickoff
   - Recommend 2-3 initiatives tied to those goals AND the trends from Section 2
   - Frame as conversation starters, not sales pitches
   - Include the key question: "What other services do you wish SOLVD provided?"
   - Prepare 3-5 open-ended questions to gather feedback

## EB Example Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Title Slide
- "Q[X] 2025 Executive Briefing for [Client Name]"
- Date, SOLVD team attendees

### Slides 2-3: Progress & Wins
- Key metrics since last quarter or project start
  - Example: "3,500 hours saved through automation"
  - Example: "Sales team adoption increased from 64% to 89%"
- Specific accomplishments: New workflow launched, integration completed, training milestone
- Team feedback: 2-3 quotes from their team members
  - "This has transformed how our sales team operates…"
  - "We're finally getting the visibility we needed…"
- Visual: Before/after process diagram, dashboard screenshot, or metric chart

### INDUSTRY INTEL & TRENDS

### Slide 4: The Big Picture
- 2-3 macro trends affecting their industry
- AI/automation adoption rates
- Regulatory changes on the horizon
- Market consolidation or competitive pressures
- One provocative insight or statistic that should be on their radar

### Slide 5: What We're Seeing in [Industry]
- Specific challenges competitors are facing
- Example innovations from adjacent industries that could apply
- Regulatory or technology shifts specific to their sector
- "Across our [industry] clients, we're consistently hearing…"

### Slide 6: Best Practices & Opportunities
- 2-3 automation or technology trends they should consider
- What leading companies in their space are prioritizing
- Cross-industry ideas that could give them competitive advantage
- Keep it practical and action-oriented

### SECTION 3: YOUR PRIORITIES + Q&A

### Slide 7: Revisiting Your Priorities
- Recap the original business objectives they shared at kickoff
- "When we started, you told us your top priorities were…"
- Check-in question: "How have these evolved?"

### Slide 8: Recommendations & Next Possibilities
- Based on Sections 1 & 2, suggest 2-3 initiatives they could consider
- Frame as: "Given what we're seeing in your industry and your goals around [X], you might want to explore…"
- Tie back to their original priorities
- Keep it consultative, not salesy

### Transition to Open Q&A:
- "We'd love to hear from you—what questions do you have about anything we've shared?"
- "What's top of mind for you right now as you think about the next 6-12 months?"
- **CRITICAL QUESTION:** "What other services or solutions do you wish SOLVD provided that we aren't offering today? If we did offer them, would you adopt them with us?"
